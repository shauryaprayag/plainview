Editorial Policy

Here's exactly how we decide what goes on the site.

Every claim on Plainview has a source you can check yourself. This page explains where we look, what we publish, what we don't, and what happens when we get something wrong. We will get things wrong. Here's how we handle it.

The basic rule

Nonpartisan by design.

Every candidate on Plainview gets the same profile layout, the same fields, and the same amount of space. That's not a design preference — it's a rule we don't break. A candidate with more donors doesn't get a bigger finance section. An incumbent with a longer record doesn't get more profile real estate. Equal structure, always.

We don't editorialize. Words like "controversial," "divisive," or "bold" won't appear in a candidate profile. Every vote, statement, and dollar figure on this site traces back to an official record or primary source. Read the profile, follow the links, and draw your own conclusions.

Where we look

The sources we trust — and why.

We only use primary sources: official records, verified public databases, and direct quotes linked back to their original publication. If something appeared in a news article that cited another source, the news article alone isn't good enough — we go find the original.

The rules

What we will and won't publish.

1
Primary sources only
Every factual claim traces back to an official record, a verified dataset, or a direct quote at its original source. Secondhand citations don't qualify.
2
No paraphrase without a citation
We don't summarize what a candidate "believes" or "opposes" without pointing to something they actually said or did. We describe the record — not what we think it means.
3
Same fields for everyone
If we can't source a field for a particular candidate, it reads "not available."
4
No loaded language
Words like "controversial," "extreme," or "divisive" have no place in a candidate profile. We describe what happened and let it speak for itself.
5
Every profile is dated
Political situations change. Every profile shows when it was last updated so you know how current the information is.
In practice

What this looks like when we write.

We write like this
Voted yes on SB 1. Source: CA Senate, Jan 2025.
Received $210k from real estate donors. Source: OpenSecrets.
Opposes Prop 34. Stated reason: fiscal impact on county budget. Source: candidate website, Oct 2025.
"Housing is a human right." — SF Chronicle, Jan 2026. [direct link]
Not like this
The senator controversially supported the divisive bill.
Is in the pocket of real estate interests.
Shockingly voted against a measure that would have helped thousands.
Sparked outrage with incendiary comments on housing policy.
When we get it wrong

We correct quickly and say so.

We will publish errors. When that happens — whether we catch it ourselves or someone points it out — we fix it fast and don't pretend it didn't happen. Corrections are logged with the date they were made, not quietly overwritten.

1
Someone flags an error — us, a reader, a campaign, or a third party. We treat all correction requests the same regardless of who's asking.
2
We check the primary source — not the correction request, the actual record. If the error is confirmed, we fix it. If it's genuinely disputed, we note the dispute without taking a side.
3
The profile is updated with accurate information and a refreshed "last updated" date.
4
Significant errors get a note. If something materially wrong was live for any meaningful period, we add a correction note to the profile indicating what changed and when.
Independence

Three things we will never do.

We will never take money from a candidate, campaign, political party, PAC, or anyone with a stake in how a candidate comes across — at any stage of growth, under any framing. No advertising, no "sponsored profiles," no exceptions.

We will never prioritize or feature a candidate based on who they are, what party they belong to, or what positions they hold. The structure is the guarantee.

We will never publish a claim we can't source. If the information isn't available, the field says so.

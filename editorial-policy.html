Editorial Policy

How we source, verify, and present information.

Every claim on Plainview comes from a public, primary source. This document explains exactly how we decide what to include, how we verify it, and what we do when we get something wrong.

Core commitment

Nonpartisan means the structure, not just the words.

Every candidate on Plainview receives an identical profile structure. The same fields, the same amount of space, the same visual weight. No candidate is featured more prominently than another. No issue is color-coded to signal urgency. The nonpartisan standard is structural, not just rhetorical.

We do not editorialize. We do not describe candidates as "controversial," "divisive," "extreme," or "popular." We report what they said, what they voted for, and where their money came from — and we link to the original source so you can verify it yourself.

Approved sources

Where our information comes from.

Plainview accepts information only from primary sources — official records, direct quotes, and verified public data. We do not accept campaign-provided summaries, press releases without corroboration, or secondary reporting as the sole basis for a claim.

Sourcing rules

What we will and won't include.

1
Primary sources only
Every factual claim must be traceable to a primary source — an official record, a verified dataset, or a direct quote linked to its original publication. Secondary reporting (e.g. "according to a news article that cited...") is not accepted as a source.
2
No paraphrase without attribution
We do not summarize what a candidate "believes" or "opposes" without a direct citation. Interpretive language is prohibited. We report what the record shows, not what we infer from it.
3
Identical structure for every candidate
Every profile contains the same fields. If a field cannot be sourced for a candidate, it is marked "not available" rather than omitted or estimated. No candidate receives a field another doesn't.
4
No editorial framing
Words like "controversial," "extreme," "popular," "divisive," and "bold" are prohibited. We describe actions and records, not character or political positioning. The reader draws conclusions; we supply facts.
5
Timestamps on all information
Every profile shows its last-updated date. Political situations change; voters deserve to know how current the information is.
Language standards

How we write — and don't write.

We write like this
Voted yes on SB 1. Source: CA Senate, Jan 2025.
Received $210k from real estate donors. Source: OpenSecrets.
Opposes Prop 34. Stated reason: fiscal impact on county budget. Source: candidate website, Oct 2025.
"Housing is a human right." — SF Chronicle, Jan 2026. [direct link]
Not like this
The senator controversially supported the divisive bill.
Is in the pocket of real estate interests.
Shockingly voted against a measure that would have helped thousands.
Sparked outrage with incendiary comments on housing policy.
Corrections

What happens when we get something wrong.

We will get things wrong. When that happens, we correct quickly and transparently. We do not silently update profiles — corrections are logged with the date they were made.

1
Error identified — by our team, a reader, a candidate campaign, or a third party. All correction requests are treated equally regardless of source.
2
Source verification — we check the claim against available primary sources. If the error is confirmed, we proceed. If disputed, we note the dispute without taking a side.
3
Correction made — the profile is updated with the accurate information. The "last updated" date is refreshed. The correction is logged internally.
4
Acknowledgment — for significant factual errors, a correction note is added to the relevant profile section indicating what changed and when.
Independence

What we will never do.

Plainview will never accept payment, advertising revenue, or sponsored content from any candidate, campaign, political party, PAC, or politically affiliated organization — at any stage of the project's growth.

We will never prioritize or feature a candidate based on who they are, what party they belong to, or what positions they hold. Our nonpartisan standard is structural and non-negotiable.

We will never publish a claim we cannot source. If information is unavailable, the field reads "not available" — not an estimate, not a guess, not a paraphrase of something we think is probably true.

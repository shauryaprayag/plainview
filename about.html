About Plainview

A clearer view of who's on your ballot.

Plainview is a nonpartisan civic technology project built to give every Bay Area voter a simple, trustworthy place to research their candidates — with no spin, no agenda, and every claim backed by a primary source.

The problem

Good information about candidates is hard to find.

Americans who want to vote informedly have no single, trustworthy place to understand what their candidates actually believe, have said, and have done. Political information is scattered across campaign websites, news archives, social media, and financial disclosures — and much of what reaches voters is filtered through emotionally charged media that prioritizes engagement over clarity.

When voters can't easily access clear, factual profiles of their candidates, they're left to navigate a landscape deliberately designed to confuse. Plainview exists to change that — lowering the effort required to become an informed voter to near zero.

What we do

Every candidate. One place. No spin.

Plainview compiles consistently structured profiles for candidates on the ballot in a given area. Each profile aggregates information that is currently scattered or inaccessible — presented in plain language, with no editorial framing.

→ Official platform & policy positions — what the candidate says they stand for, in their own words.
→ Voting record — for incumbents, sourced directly from Congress.gov or official legislative records.
→ Press statements — things candidates have said publicly, with direct links to the original source.
→ Campaign finance — where their money comes from, via OpenSecrets and FEC public data.
→ Endorsements — organizations and individuals who have publicly backed the candidate.
→ Stance on key issues — housing, immigration, climate, and other high-relevance topics, sourced from primary records.
Our principles

Built on five commitments.

Informed
Visitors leave with the full picture — sourced, structured, and in plain language. No detail is buried.
Nonpartisan
No issue, party, or candidate gets more visual weight than another. Color, layout, and language are all held to this standard.
Human
Warm, approachable, communal. Built for every Bay Area voter — not just the politically engaged.
Professional
Editorial credibility. All claims cite primary sources. The site looks and feels like something you can trust.
Simple but thorough
Every layer of detail is earned. Information density should never feel overwhelming.
Private
No personal data collected. No tracking, no sign-in, no ads. Plainview is read-only and always will be.
What we are not

A few things Plainview deliberately isn't.

— We are not a news organization. We do not report, editorialize, or take positions on candidates or issues.
— We are not affiliated with any political party, campaign, PAC, or government agency.
— We are not Ballotpedia. Ballotpedia is built for policy researchers. Plainview is built for the first-time voter doing research the night before an election.
— We do not accept advertising, sponsored content, or paid placements of any kind.
Who built this

A student civic tech project.

Plainview is a project developed under the National Student Council's Glisten program, which supports student-led civic innovation. It began as a Bay Area pilot with a small set of candidates, with the goal of expanding to all of California and eventually becoming a national resource.

The project is maintained by a small team of student researchers committed to accuracy, fairness, and the belief that an informed vote is a more powerful vote.
